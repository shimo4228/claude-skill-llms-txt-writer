# What 1.2 Million ChatGPT Responses Reveal About Citation Patterns

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is defined as the discipline of structuring content so that large language models such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini choose to cite it. Brand authority correlates with citation rate at r=0.334 in the Kevin Indig study of 1.2M ChatGPT answers.

## Why does positional bias matter?

The Stanford "Lost in the Middle" paper shows LLMs systematically underweight mid-document information. In the Indig analysis, 44.2% of citations come from the first 30% of a document — a pattern called the "ski-ramp" distribution.

## Structural features

Content split into 50-150 word self-contained chunks receives roughly 2.3x more citations. Structured formats (bullets, tables, statistics) outperform plain paragraphs by a factor of three. Headings written as questions — such as "Why does X matter?" — increase citation rate by 2.8x.

## Entity density

Citation-worthy passages contain about 20.6% named entities (brands, tools, people), versus 5-8% for standard English text. Definitional language ("is defined as", "refers to") correlates with a 36.2% citation rate, compared to 20.2% for vague phrasing.
